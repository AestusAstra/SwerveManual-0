differential swerve, instead of having an individual azimuth rotation actuator via servo, and movement powered via motor, it utilizes a differential gear system in order to acheive azimuth rotation as well as wheel rotation. moving the larger two parallel bevel gears in opposite directions at the same speed enables directional movement via the rotation of the small bevel gear connected to a shaft, while mvoing them in the same speed enables rapid azimuth rotation.

Pros:
no servos required
can handle a heavier robot as azimuth rotation has more power
faster azimuth rotation
less current draw
cons:
only has 2 wheels when 4 motors used, meaning less traction than coax typically or its crab diffy, in which turning speed and current draw are worse
worse spacing, and more difficult to package
can be more complex
